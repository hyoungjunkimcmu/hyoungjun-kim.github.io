I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Prof. Vanessa Chen. My research focuses on Processing-in-Sensor (PIS), Deep Learning Hardware, and Energy-Efficient Mixed-Signal Circuits.

Research Interests

  • Processing-in-Sensor (PIS)
  • Energy-Efficient Mixed-Signal Circuits
  • Deep Learning Hardware
  • VLSI System Design
  • Sensor-Integrated Machine Learning

Short Bio

Before joining CMU, I received my M.S. and B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU), Korea, where I worked on LiDAR SoC design and mixed-signal systems under Prof. Jaehyuk Choi.

I also worked at Samsung Electronics (DRAM Design Team, Memory Division) as a circuit design engineer, focusing on DC-chain circuits (BGR, TCSR, LDO) for high-bandwidth memory (HBM3E/HBM4).
